Thomas Edison

The great inventor of humanity

Here's a time line of Thomas Edison's life:

Early Life (1847-1870)

  • 1847 - Born in Milan, Ohio
  • 1854 - Family moves to Port Huron, Michigan
  • 1859 - Starts working as a newsboy and telegraph operator
  • 1863 - Obtains a full-time job with the Grand Trunk Railway as a telegraph operator

Early Inventions and Marriage (1870-1880)

  • 1870 - Opens his first workshop in Newark, New Jersey
  • 1871 - Marries Mary Stilwell
  • 1873 - Daughter Marion is born
  • 1876 - Develops the carbon-resistance telephone transmitter (important for telephone technology)
  • 1877 - Creates the phonograph, the first device to record and reproduce sound
  • 1879 - Invents the incandescent light bulb, revolutionizing lighting technology

Building an Empire (1880-1900)

  • 1880 - Discovers the "Edison Effect", which forms the basis of the electronic tube
  • 1882 - Opens the world's first commercial electric power station in New York City (Pearl Street Station)
  • 1884 - Mary, his wife, dies
  • 1886 - Marries Mina Miller and establishes West Orange laboratory in New Jersey
  • 1887 - Develops the "New Phonograph" using a wax cylinder for recording sound
  • 1891 - Invents the kinetoscope, a precursor to the motion picture projectortube

Later Life and Legacy (1900-1931)

  • 1903 - Founds the Edison Illuminating Company, a major electric utility company
  • 1915 - Awarded the Congressional Gold Medal for his contributions to society
  • 1931 - Dies in West Orange, New Jersey
